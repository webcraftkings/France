Professional Purpose

Engaging in a professional activity in France is subject to different formalities depending on your situation—whether you're self-employed, an employee, your business type, and your length of stay.

As a Self-Employed Person

  • Come to France for business to meet your commercial and professional obligations.
  • Want to start or take part in a self-employed or liberal activity.

As an Employee

  • Be posted by your employer to provide a service.
  • Participate in intra-group mobility while remaining contractually attached to your employer as an ICT (Intra Corporate Transferee) or with a local contract in France.
  • You've been hired for a fixed or indefinite period by a French employer. This may include seasonal contracts.
  • Take part in a professional course in a training organisation or company.

Important:

Certain professions (e.g. doctors, teachers) have specific conditions to meet before they can practice.

If you're contributing to France’s economic appeal as an employee or self-employed individual—an international talent—you can also make arrangements accordingly.
